Two examples of procedurally generated islands with probability

This is an expansion on the previous idea of procedural island generation, you may want to check out my previous post for procedural island generation

Side note: The beige color tiles are sand tiles that get added upon finalization. They do not represent the candidate positions. I really like them because they help establish that 'island' vibe

In this version of my procedural generation project, the chance of a candidate position becoming a land tile is determined by it's surrounding tiles. Land tiles and candidate position tiles are both considered as neighbours.

Below is a diagram that represents a candidate position and it's adjacent tiles. White squares represent free spaces to the candidate. Black squares represent neighbouring squares to the candidate

from left to right: 'one sided' piece, 'corner' piece, 'line' piece, 'crevice' piece

If a candidate position has one side where it meets a neightbour, and three sides where it does not meet neither a land tile nor a candidate position tile, it is considered to be a 'one side' piece. Refer image 1

If a candidate position has two sides where it meets a neightbour, it may fall under one of two categories, either a 'line' piece or 'corner' piece.

A piece is a corner piece when the neighbours are on adjacent sides of the candidate. Refer image 2

A piece is a line piece when the neighbours are on opposite sides of the candidate. Refer image 3

A piece is a crevice piece when there are three neighbours and one free side. Refer image 4

The probability manipulation of each type of candidate peace results in different types of islands For example, if there is a low probability of 'one-sided' pieces converting to land tiles, this will result in minimal protruding land pieces.

Examples of solid well shaped islands

Contrasting that, if there is a high probability of 'one-sided' pieces converting to land tiles, this will result in an island with many peninsulas. They will also have very erratic shapes.

Examples of islands with alot of peninsulas

Controls

Left click: Run a round of tile generation
Right click: Fix unchosen candidates inside island
Space bar: Reset generation
Scroll in/out: Zoom in/out

1: +10% probability for one-sided pieces
2: +10% probability for corner pieces
3: +10% probability for line pieces
4: +10% probability for crevice pieces

Shift + 1: -10% probability for one-sided pieces
Shift + 2: -10% probability for corner pieces
Shift + 3: -10% probability for line pieces
Shift + 4: -10% probability for crevice pieces

The first number on the top corner indicates whether a click is registered or not

The second number on the top corner indicates the amount of possible candidates for land tiles to generate on.

The numbers below the potential candidates show the probability of each piece type spawning
